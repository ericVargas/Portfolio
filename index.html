About Me

Once as a child, my uncle gifted me a whole desktop computer. Not a prebuilt one from the store but separate individual components and a Windows XP disc. Together we assembled it and installed the OS, I was instantly hook into technology and programming.

That passion led me to choose my high school of Information Technology. I was given the opportunity to take classes of computer hardware, basic web development with Dreamweaver and animation with Flash (both programs were owned by Macromedia before Adobe bought them along with Fireworks).

Later on in college I studied the art of Game Design and Development where I was introduced to Visual Basic, C#, and C++. A few years later, I was accepted into TechHire Open Code, an immersive course into web development in conjunction with LaGuardia Community College and General Assembly. There I learned the fundamentals of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript; with NodeJS for Backend and React for Frontend.
